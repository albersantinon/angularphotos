About AngularPhotos

Frame 05 / 36 · Contact Sheet Notes

AngularPhotos is a photography and videography practice orbiting the edges of live music, street light and quiet in–between moments. The work leans into grain, blur and the small gestures that usually slip between frames.

The lens moves between backstage corridors, late–night sidewalks and small venues where the monitors hum and the crowd becomes a soft blur. Digital cameras are treated like loaded film bodies—every frame considered, every sequence built like a song.

Influenced by 90s underground flyers, 35mm contact sheets and a slight Japanese sensibility for negative space, AngularPhotos builds visual stories that feel lived–in, imperfect and quietly cinematic.

“Rooms That Remember Sound”

A cinematic live session following an indie band across rehearsal spaces, cramped stages and the quiet walks home after the last chord fades.

Built as a hybrid between documentary and music video, the project leans into handheld motion, soft focus and the way stage lights bleed into the crowd. Each sequence is treated like a track on a record: opening, build, chorus, comedown. The camera stays close enough to feel the monitors hum.
